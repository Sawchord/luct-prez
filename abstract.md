# A decade in certificate transparency and what may come next

## Abstract 

This talk revisits the evolution of the Certificate Transparency (CT) protocol, beginning with a brief recap of the problem that motivated its design. 

The talk will examine the current state of the ecosystem, including browser enforcement policies and current log operators, as well as recent developments such as the static CT API rollout.

After that, I'll highlight the remaining gaps in the security of the protocol, such as issues with log list management and the lack of progress on gossip.

In the last section, I'll introduce luCT, a project I am working on, which attempts to tackle some of the remaining issues in the CT ecosystem before closing with an outlook into the future of the ecosystem and a call to action.

## Description

Over the last decace, certificate transparency (CT) has become an integral part of the web's security infrastructure.

However, the story of CT is far from finished.

In this talk, I want to unpack where CT stands today, what has been achieved during the last 10 years, which issues the ecosystem is still struggling with today and what we may be able do about it.
